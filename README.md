# Cybertarr

**Cybertarr** is an experimental developmental cognitive architecture designed to investigate whether intelligence can emerge from interacting computational systems rather than a single monolithic neural network.

Unlike traditional AI systems that begin with large-scale training, Cybertarr starts as a **Digital Embryo** with no knowledge of language, objects, mathematics, or the world.

The project explores intelligence as a developmental process driven by observation, internal state, memory, learning, and adaptation.

## Research Philosophy

Cybertarr follows a scientific workflow:

1. Form a hypothesis.
2. Design an architecture.
3. Implement the feature.
4. Run experiments.
5. Measure results.
6. Accept or reject the hypothesis.

The objective is not to reproduce biology exactly, but to investigate whether biological principles can inspire new computational architectures.

## Long-Term Vision

Cybertarr aims to become a research platform for developmental artificial intelligence, continual learning, and brain-inspired computation.
