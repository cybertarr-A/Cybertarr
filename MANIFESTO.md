# Cybertarr Manifesto

> **Version:** 1.0
> **Status:** Active

---

# Introduction

Cybertarr is not simply a software project.

It is a long-term scientific and engineering effort to investigate developmental artificial intelligence.

The objective is not to produce another chatbot or language model.

The objective is to understand whether intelligence can emerge from a computational architecture that develops over time.

Every contributor is expected to prioritize scientific integrity, engineering quality, and reproducibility over speed.

---

# Mission

Our mission is to build a developmental cognitive architecture that begins with minimal capabilities and acquires increasingly complex behavior through interaction with its environment.

We seek understanding before optimization.

We seek evidence before conclusions.

---

# Core Values

## Curiosity

Every component exists to answer a scientific question.

Questions are more valuable than assumptions.

---

## Scientific Integrity

No hypothesis is considered true until experimental evidence supports it.

Every major architectural decision should be testable.

If evidence contradicts a hypothesis, the hypothesis changes.

The evidence does not.

---

## Simplicity

Complex systems should emerge from simple mechanisms whenever possible.

Do not introduce unnecessary abstraction.

Do not introduce unnecessary biological detail.

Every additional component increases complexity and must justify its existence.

---

## Transparency

Every important decision made by Cybertarr should be explainable.

Researchers should be able to inspect:

* Internal state
* Memory
* Decision paths
* Development history
* Experimental results

Black-box behavior should be minimized whenever practical.

---

## Modularity

Every subsystem should have one clear responsibility.

Subsystems communicate through well-defined interfaces.

Changing one subsystem should not require rewriting the entire architecture.

---

# Engineering Principles

## Code follows architecture.

Architecture never follows code.

Design first.

Implement second.

---

## Every feature begins with documentation.

Before implementation, every major feature requires:

* Architecture Specification (CAS)
* Hypothesis
* Experiment Design

---

## Every feature must be measurable.

If success cannot be measured, the feature is incomplete.

---

## Build incrementally.

Do not attempt to solve intelligence in one step.

Solve one research question at a time.

---

## Keep the system observable.

Researchers should always be able to understand what Cybertarr is doing internally.

Development is easier when the system can explain itself.

---

# Research Workflow

Every feature follows the same lifecycle.

1. Identify a research question.
2. Write the hypothesis.
3. Design the architecture.
4. Implement the smallest working prototype.
5. Execute experiments.
6. Measure results.
7. Review conclusions.
8. Decide whether to keep, improve, or remove the feature.

---

# Documentation Standards

Every experiment should include:

* Objective
* Hypothesis
* Implementation
* Experimental Procedure
* Results
* Observations
* Conclusion
* Open Questions

Every architectural change should include:

* Motivation
* Alternatives Considered
* Advantages
* Disadvantages
* Expected Impact

---

# Coding Standards

The codebase should prioritize:

* Readability
* Correctness
* Maintainability
* Performance
* Safety

Code should be understandable before it is optimized.

Premature optimization is discouraged.

---

# Experimentation

Experiments exist to discover knowledge.

A failed experiment is not a failed project.

Negative results are valuable and should be documented.

The architecture should evolve based on evidence rather than preference.

---

# Long-Term Philosophy

Cybertarr is expected to evolve over many years.

The project should remain flexible enough to replace or redesign any subsystem if evidence suggests a better approach.

No implementation is permanent.

Only the scientific process is permanent.

---

# Definition of Success

Success is not measured solely by benchmark scores or model size.

Success means improving our understanding of computational intelligence.

Examples include:

* Discovering better learning mechanisms.
* Demonstrating continual adaptation.
* Building reproducible cognitive experiments.
* Publishing verifiable research.
* Creating an extensible architecture for future researchers.

---

# Contributor Oath

Every contributor agrees to:

* Respect evidence over opinion.
* Keep experiments reproducible.
* Document important decisions.
* Write understandable code.
* Challenge assumptions.
* Accept constructive criticism.
* Improve the architecture through careful experimentation.

---

# Closing Statement

Cybertarr is an exploration of intelligence.

Every line of code should answer a question.

Every experiment should teach us something.

Every failure should improve our understanding.

The goal is not merely to build an intelligent machine.

The goal is to understand how intelligence itself can emerge.
