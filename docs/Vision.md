# Why does Cybertarr exist?

Artificial intelligence has made remarkable progress through large-scale training and increasingly powerful neural network architectures. However, most modern systems begin as highly capable models that are trained before they ever interact with the world.

Cybertarr explores a different research direction.

Instead of asking:

> *"How can we train a larger model?"*

Cybertarr asks:

> *"Can intelligence emerge through development?"*

The project begins with a **Digital Embryo**—a computational organism possessing no knowledge of language, mathematics, objects, or the external world. Its purpose is not to answer questions immediately, but to develop capabilities through interaction with its environment.

Cybertarr is therefore not intended to be "another chatbot." It is an experimental developmental cognitive architecture designed to investigate alternative approaches to artificial intelligence.

# Scientific Questions

The project is organized around research questions rather than predetermined conclusions.

Current questions include:

## Development

* Can a cognitive system begin with almost no knowledge?
* Which capabilities must exist at birth?
* Which capabilities should emerge over time?

## Observation

* What is the smallest meaningful unit of experience?
* How should observations influence internal state?
* When does an observation become significant?

## Memory

* How do repeated observations become memories?
* Which memories should be retained?
* Which memories should be forgotten?

## Learning

* Can concepts emerge from repeated experiences?
* Can continual learning occur without catastrophic forgetting?
* Can knowledge grow without complete retraining?

## Reasoning

* Can planning emerge from memory and prediction?
* How should uncertainty influence decision making?
* What computational mechanisms support flexible reasoning?

## Architecture

* Can a distributed cognitive architecture outperform or complement monolithic systems for certain tasks?
* Which biological ideas are computationally useful?
* Which biological ideas are unnecessary?

These questions will evolve as the project progresses.

# Core Principles

Every design decision should respect these principles.

## 1. Intelligence is developed, not installed.

Capabilities should emerge through interaction, learning, or explicit architectural mechanisms rather than arbitrary feature additions.

---

## 2. Observation precedes understanding.

The system should experience the world before attempting to interpret it.

---

## 3. The world exists independently of the mind.

The environment generates observations.

The cognitive architecture does not invent its own external experiences.

---

## 4. Simplicity before complexity.

A simple mechanism that explains behavior is preferred over a complex mechanism with the same outcome.

---

## 5. Every architectural decision requires evidence.

Ideas are valuable.

Evidence is more valuable.

Major claims should be supported by experiments whenever possible.

---

## 6. Modularity over monoliths.

Independent systems with clearly defined responsibilities are preferred over tightly coupled implementations.

---

## 7. Transparency by design.

Every important decision made by Cybertarr should be inspectable by researchers.

The project should prioritize understanding over opacity.

---

## 8. Scientific honesty.

If an experiment disproves one of our hypotheses, the architecture should change.

The project exists to discover what works—not to defend preconceived ideas.

---

# Long-Term Vision

Over the next five to ten years, Cybertarr aims to become a research platform for developmental artificial intelligence.

Success is not defined by creating a chatbot or achieving a specific benchmark.

Instead, success means producing new, reproducible knowledge about cognitive architectures.

Examples of meaningful success include:

* Demonstrating effective continual learning.
* Developing robust long-term memory mechanisms.
* Discovering new approaches to concept formation.
* Creating modular cognitive systems that can be independently studied and improved.
* Publishing reproducible research based on experimental evidence.

Whether or not Cybertarr ultimately reaches human-level intelligence, advancing our understanding of computational cognition would represent a successful outcome.

---

# Research Philosophy

Cybertarr follows an iterative scientific process.

1. Observe a problem.
2. Form a hypothesis.
3. Design an architecture.
4. Implement a prototype.
5. Perform experiments.
6. Measure results.
7. Accept, modify, or reject the hypothesis.
8. Repeat.

Every iteration should improve either the architecture or our understanding of intelligence.

---

# Final Statement

Cybertarr is an exploration of developmental intelligence.

Its purpose is not to imitate the human brain in every biological detail, nor to replace existing AI architectures. Its purpose is to investigate whether intelligence can emerge from a system that begins with minimal capabilities, develops through interaction, and evolves according to experimentally validated principles.

Every line of code should contribute to answering a scientific question.

Every experiment should improve our understanding.

Every failure should teach us something.
