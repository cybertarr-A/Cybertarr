# Entry 0001

**Date:** July 13, 2026

**Researcher:** Cybertarr Research Lab (Founder: Cybertarr-A)

## Hypothesis

A developmental cognitive architecture should begin as a minimal computational organism rather than as a fully trained intelligence.

The first capability of such an organism is not reasoning or language, but the ability to maintain a continuous internal lifecycle ("heartbeat") and preserve a valid internal state over time.

## Motivation

Most AI systems are created after extensive training on massive datasets.

Cybertarr explores a different hypothesis:

Intelligence may be better understood as a developmental process where increasingly complex behavior emerges from simple interacting mechanisms.

## Implementation

Implemented the first Digital Embryo prototype in Rust.

Current capabilities:

* Internal state
* Continuous heartbeat
* Tick counter
* Persistent execution loop
* State visualization

Current limitations:

* No observations
* No memory
* No learning
* No planning
* No goals
* No language

## Experiment

Procedure:

1. Start the Digital Embryo.
2. Execute continuous brain ticks.
3. Verify that the internal state remains valid.
4. Verify that the heartbeat continues without interruption.

## Observations

The embryo successfully maintained continuous execution.

Each heartbeat incremented the internal age counter while preserving a consistent internal state.

The architecture successfully demonstrated the concept of a persistent computational organism.

## Conclusion

The first milestone has been achieved.

The Digital Embryo now possesses a stable computational lifecycle.

Future development should focus on introducing observation before implementing memory or learning.

## Next Research Question

What is the smallest computational mechanism required for an observation to become a persistent internal representation?
